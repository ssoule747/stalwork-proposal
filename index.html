The Challenge

The tools aren't keeping up
with the operation.

3 PMs. 12+ self-performed trades per project. Concurrent luxury builds across the Central Coast. The operation is sophisticated — the systems running it are not.

01

Three PMs. No shared dashboard.

Ben, James, and Frank each run concurrent builds with 10+ trades happening simultaneously. Project status lives across texts, emails, and spreadsheets. Nobody sees the full picture.

02

Seven-figure clients. Reactive updates.

Client communication runs on phone calls and emails. A family investing seven figures in a custom home shouldn't have to call and ask, "What's happening this week?"

03

6 open positions. 2 admin staff.

Cortney and Abigail support every project, every client, every invoice. Every new hire multiplies communication complexity. Without a central system, scaling creates chaos.

04

Specialized crews. No shared schedule.

Pablo's concrete crew, Aaron's framers, Omar's tile team — all moving between job sites across a 35-mile radius. Scheduling conflicts don't announce themselves. They show up when someone's on-site with no crew.

05

Permits and inspections. Across every project.

Title 24, arborist certs, grading permits, multi-agency approvals — all on overlapping timelines across concurrent builds. One missed inspection means work stops. That tracking can't live in someone's memory.

The operation has outgrown the tools. The question is whether the replacement gets built around Stalwork — or whether Stalwork has to bend to fit something off the shelf.
